Great Ocean Road Attractions

Great Ocean Road

An Australian National Heritage listed 240 kilometre stretch of road along the south-eastern coast of Australia, along one of the worldâ€™s most scenic coastal drives between the Victorian towns of Torquay and Allansford near Warnambool. A prime tourist attraction, it provides extensive views over the Bass Strait and the Southern Ocean. The road runs through rainforests, as well as alongside beaches and cliffs composed of limestone and sandstone such as the world famous Twelve Apostles. It travels via towns such as Anglesea, Lorne, Apollo Bay, and Port Campbell.

1.  Anglesea

A Small town just 10 minutes west of Torquay and marks the first point where the road meets the coast. It's a popular tourist spot
with surf and swimming beaches surrounded by beautiful forest and coastal scenery.It has an estimated population of 3,208 people
from the 2021 census

2.  Lorne

Located betwwen Aireys Inlet and Wyre River on one of the most scenic sections of the Great Ocean Road,
Lorne is a very popular tourist town with an estimated population of 1,327 people from the
2021 census but this number increases substantially during the holiday season. A popular attraction
in the area is Teddys lookout which has breathtaking views of the ocean and the Great Ocean Road.
It's located at the back of Lorne and is just a short drive to the top end of George Street.

3.   Apollo Bay

Located at the foothills of the Otways and to the east of Great Otway National Park, Apollo Bay is a coastal town and is
a major tourist destination in Victoria. It has an estimated population of 2,062 from the 2021 census although
that number is higher during the holiday season. There are museums such as the Bass Straight Shell Museum and the Old
Cable Staion Museum located in Apollo Bay and is host to the annual Apollo Bay Seafood Festival, Winter Wild and the Great
Ocean Road Running Festival. A good lookout spot located on the outskirts of the town is the Marriner Lookout that overlooks
Apollo Bay. Apollo Bay is a major fishing town along the Great Ocean Road and a good place to visit is the Apollo Bay Fishermenâ€™s
Co-Op which has a restaurant that offers fresh seafood. It has been around for more than 70 years and the last working fishing
habour on the Great Ocean Road is located here and offers good views of the town and the surrounding area.
