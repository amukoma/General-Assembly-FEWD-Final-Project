Great Ocean Road Attractions

4. Cape Otway Lightstation

Is situated at the southern tip of Victoria's western coast, where the Southern Ocean meets Bass Strait. Much of the district
around Cape Otway is included in the Great Otway National Park, which features rainforest and streams in the Otway Ranges,
and extends right down to the coast. Built in 1848, Cape Otway Lighthouse is the oldest surviving lighthouse on mainland Australia and sits
90 metres above sea level. For many thousands of 19th century migrants, who spent months travellingto Australia by ship, Cape Otway
was their first sight of land. Visitors can climb to the top of the lighthouse for spectacular views and tour the surrounding historical buildings.

5. The Twelve Apostles

The most popular attraction along the route and a world famous attraction, they are a collection of limestone stacks located off the shore
of Port Campbell National Park. Wind and sea erosion continually batters them, with some having collapsed over the years. Its located east of the Great Otway National Park.
There's options of exploring the national park by foot along numerous short walks that end with spectacular lookouts and also reef and shipwreck diving along off an area known as the Shipwreck Coast.
Other attractions in the area include London Bridge, the Loch Ard Gorge and Gibsons Steps.

6. Port Campbell

Is a small scenic coastal town located 12 kilometers west of the Twelve Apostles and the Port Campbell National Park.
The town is popular due to the proximity to the Twelve Apostles. The town has a population of 440 people from the 2021 census. It has numerous restaurants and cafes with views of the beach with many shops to explore
